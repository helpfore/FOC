NA Derivation — Step Index

Ray enters end face at θₐ; TIR must occur at core-cladding interface:

① n₀ sin θₐ = n₁ sin θ₁ (end face, n₀=1)
② n₁ sin θc = n₂ → TIR condition
③ θ₁ = 90°−θc → sin θ₁ = cos θc
④ cos θc = √(1−n₂²/n₁²)
∴ NA = sin θₐ = √(n₁²−n₂²)
NA = n₁√(2Δ) [since Δ≈(n₁²−n₂²)/2n₁²]
V = (2πa/λ)·NA = (πd/λ)·NA
SM conditionV < 2.405
MM-SI modes M≈ V²/2
MM-GI modes M≈ V²/4

Electromagnetic Mode Theory & LP Modes

EM Modes in Fiber:

  • TE modes: Ez=0; transverse electric field only
  • TM modes: Hz=0; transverse magnetic field only
  • HE modes: Hybrid; dominant mode = HE₁₁ (no cutoff)
  • EH modes: Hybrid; Hz dominates over Ez
β = (2π/λ)·n_eff [propagation constant]
Guided mode conditionn₂k < β < n₁k
Mode field dia (SM)9–10 µm (2×spot size w)

Linearly Polarized (LP) Modes:

  • Weakly guiding (n₁≈n₂): TE+TM+HE grouped as LP modes
  • LP₀₁ ≡ HE₁₁ — fundamental, no cutoff
  • LP₁₁ ≡ TE₀₁+TM₀₁+HE₂₁ — cutoff at V=2.405
  • LP_lm cutoff: J_{l-1}(Vc)=0
  • Each LP mode has 2 polarization states (degenerate)
SM: only LP₀₁(HE₁₁) propagates when V < 2.405
Cutoff λcλc = 2πa·NA / 2.405

PCVD & OVD/VAD Methods

PCVD (Plasma CVD):

  • Microwave plasma (~2.45 GHz) inside tube; no external torch
  • Low pressure → direct vitrification; no soot; very thin layers (1–10 µm)
  • Precise profile; ~100% deposition efficiency
  • Disadvantage: Expensive microwave equipment

OVD (Outside Vapour Deposition):

  • Soot deposited on outside of rotating mandrel (flame hydrolysis)
  • Mandrel removed → porous preform sintered; large preforms possible

VAD (Vapour Axial Deposition):

  • Soot deposited axially at bottom of growing preform; continuous process

Attenuation — Types & Mechanisms

Absorption Loss:

  • Intrinsic UV: Electronic transitions in silica; dominant at <1µm
  • Intrinsic IR: Molecular vibrations; tail extends to 1550nm region
  • Extrinsic — Metal ions: Fe, Cu, Mn even at ppb cause significant loss
  • Extrinsic — OH⁻: Absorption peaks at 950, 1240, 1380nm; "dry fiber" <1ppb OH

Scattering Loss:

  • Rayleigh scattering: Microscopic density fluctuations (frozen during manufacture); loss ∝ 1/λ⁴; sets 0.2dB/km floor at 1550nm
  • Mie scattering: Inhomogeneities ≈ λ size; imperfections in core; less wavelength dependent
  • Brillouin/Raman: Nonlinear scattering at high power

Bending (Radiative) Loss:

  • Macrobend: Large radius bend; outer field must travel faster → radiates; exponential increase as R decreases; min R≈25–30mm
  • Microbend: Tiny random bends (manufacturing, cabling pressure); couples guided→radiation modes; reduced by proper buffering/jacketing
α(dB/km) = (10/L)·log₁₀(Pᵢₙ/Pₒᵤₜ)

Semiconductor Physics Background

Energy Bands:

  • Conduction band (CB): Electrons; above Fermi level
  • Valence band (VB): Holes; below Fermi level
  • Bandgap Eg: Forbidden region between CB and VB
  • Emitted photon: hν = Eg (wavelength λ = hc/Eg)
λ(µm) = 1.24/Eg(eV)

Emission Types:

  • Spontaneous: Random electron-hole recombination; incoherent; LED basis
  • Stimulated: Incident photon triggers recombination; coherent copy; laser basis
  • Absorption: Photon absorbed; electron jumps to CB
Stimulated: hν_in → E₂ → E₁ + 2hν (coherent)

Population Inversion & Recombination

Population Inversion: N₂ > N₁ (more electrons in excited state than ground state)

  • Normal: N₁ > N₂ (absorption dominates)
  • Inversion: N₂ > N₁ (stimulated emission dominates) → optical gain
  • Cannot achieve in 2-level system
  • Need 3-level or 4-level pumping scheme

In Semiconductor: Forward bias injects carriers → fills CB → population inversion in active region above threshold current Ith

1/τ = 1/τᵣ + 1/τ_nr
ηᵢ = τ/τᵣ = τ_nr/(τᵣ+τ_nr)
τᵣ (radiative)~1–10 ns

Laser Diode — Modes, Threshold & Rate Equations

Threshold Conditions for Lasing:

  • Gain ≥ Loss: Optical gain must exceed all cavity losses (absorption, scatter, transmission)
  • Phase condition: Round-trip phase = 2πm (m = integer) → standing wave modes
  • Threshold current Ith: Minimum current for lasing; above Ith output increases sharply
Threshold gain: g_th = α_int + (1/2L)·ln(1/R₁R₂)

System Design Considerations
  • Dispersion limit: Max distance set by dispersion: L_disp = 1/(D·Δλ·B)
  • Attenuation limit: Max distance set by loss: L_att = (Pt−Ps−penalties)/αf
  • Ruling limit: Min(L_disp, L_att) determines reach
  • Power penalty: Extra power needed due to dispersion, reflections, modal noise
  • ORL: Optical return loss; back-reflections from connectors degrade laser stability
Chapter 6 — Optical Amplifiers
EDFA — Architecture & Mechanism
Signal(1550nm) ──[WDM Coupler]──[Er³⁺ Fiber 10–50m]──[Isolator]── Amp. signal ↑ Pump Laser (980nm or 1480nm)

Energy Level Mechanism:

  • 980nm pump: Er³⁺ E₁→E₃ (excited); fast non-rad decay E₃→E₂
  • E₂ (metastable): Long lifetime ~10 ms; population inversion builds
  • Signal triggers: Stimulated emission E₂→E₁; 1530–1565nm amplified
